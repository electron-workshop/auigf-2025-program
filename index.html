auIGF 2025 Program

All times below are Adelaide time, ACST (UTC+9:30).
Speakers are confirmed at the time of publishing, but may be subject to change.

Day 1: Tuesday, 23 September 2025

Time Program Description
9:00 – 10:00AM
Registration

Collect your badge and enjoy some morning tea while meeting fellow participants.
10:00 – 11:30AM
Opening Plenary

A Welcome to Country and remarks from the Chair lead into a keynote speech from international Internet Governance expert and ICANN Board Member Chris Buckridge.

A multi-stakeholder panel will then unpack the event’s big themes and invite audience reflection. The session will help to set Australia’s Internet governance agenda for 2025.

Panellists:

  • Michael Brown MP – South Australia’s Assistant Minister for Artificial Intelligence and the Digital Economy
  • Johanna Weaver – Tech Policy Design Institute (TPDi)
  • Christine Runnegar – Internet Society
  • Rohan Sachdeva – youth voice

MC:

  • Jordan Carter – auDA
11:40AM – 12:40PM
The Commonwealth in Conversation: WSIS+20 Reflections

Australia's negotiating team will take stock of two decades of the WSIS framework and highlight priorities for Australia as the WSIS+20 review approaches. In this dynamic session, expect insights into negotiation dynamics and to have your questions answered.

Speakers:

  • William Lee – Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development, Communications, Sport and the Arts
  • Cheryl Langdon-Orr – Internet Australia

Sponsored by the Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development, Communications, Sport and the Arts
12:40 – 1:40PM
Lunch Break

Lunchtime session available for those who wish to grab some lunch and listen in.
12:50 – 1:20PM
Lunch Session

Sovereignty in the Digital Age: Reclaiming Identity, Governance, and Power

This session explores digital sovereignty, and how uniting intent, consent, and governance in digital infrastructure can reclaim power from extractive models and seed a plural, regenerative digital future for all society.

Speakers:

  • Simon O'Toole – Alliance ICT, Leap Foundation & Dreaming Machines
  • Alexar Pendashteh – Linux Victoria & Goodness Triangle
1:40 – 2:40PM
No One Left Behind – Digital Inclusion & AI Equity

Everyone in Australia deserves the chance to succeed in today’s digital society. With 6.6 million Australians lacking capacity to properly participate in online life, this panel will explore the imperative for national action on digital inclusion, covering key examples of strategy, leadership, and opportunities for policy to help deliver broader benefit.

Speakers:

  • Dane Glerum – Good Things Australia
  • Dr Kim Osman – Digital Media Research Centre, Queensland University of Technology
  • Heather Rea – Telstra
  • Nicole Rossini – NSW Telco Authority

Moderator:

  • Tim Marshall – Australian Digital Inclusion Alliance

Sponsored by ICANN
2:50 – 3:50PM
Digital Platform Ownership – Diversity, Resilience & Trust

New data reveal Australia’s Internet supply chain is dominated by key global tech giants. Panellists examine what this concentration means for media diversity, democratic resilience and public trust, and ask whether the firms are now both “too big to fail” and “too big to govern”.

Speakers:

  • Cameron McTernan – The University of South Australia
  • Terry Flew – The University of Sydney
  • Mr Ram Mohan – Identity Digital

Moderator:

  • Victoria Fielding – The University of Adelaide

Sponsored by Identity Digital
3:50 – 4:15PM
Afternoon Tea
4:15 – 5:15PM
Multi‑stakeholder Ways to Improve Trust in Australia’s Digital Ecosystem

Co-regulation in the communications sector is under pressure. Consumers criticise it, and the trend is towards more direct regulation. Could multi-stakeholder methods help solve problems and reduce the need for regulation? Could lessons in this sector be applied more broadly on Internet issues?

Speakers:

  • Luke Coleman – Australian Telecommunications Alliance
  • Carol Bennett – Australian Communications Consumer Action Network (ACCAN)
  • Sam Grunhard – Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development, Communications, Sport and the Arts
  • Bruce Tonkin – auDA
  • Zoe Hawkins – Tech Policy Design Institute (TPDi)

Moderator:

  • Narelle Clark – Internet Association of Australia (IAA)

Sponsored by the Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development, Communications, Sport and the Arts
5:15 – 6:30PM
Networking Event sponsored by Verisign

Networking drinks will be served at the venue immediately after the final session concludes.

Day 2: Wednesday, 24 September 2025

Time Program Description
9:00 – 10:00AM
Town Hall – Position Paper

This open forum is an opportunity for sharing views and feedback on the draft auIGF 2025 position papers circulated in advance of the auIGF. Attendees can test general views of the participants as a whole about whether the direction set out is agreeable to most.

Papers under discussion:

  • The proposal to explore and develop a Social Contract for Digital Wellbeing in Australia (new paper for 2025)
  • An update to our 2024 paper for the WSIS+20 review

This session will be led and moderated by auIGF Multi-Stakeholder Steering Committee members.
10:00 – 10:30AM
Morning Tea
